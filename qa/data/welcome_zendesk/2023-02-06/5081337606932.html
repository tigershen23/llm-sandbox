Screen Sharing on Firefox Mac OSX

Screen sharing from Firefox on a Mac is possible, but not our recommended way to share (we recommend using Chrome whenever possible). You will probably either be screen sharing a slide-based presentation such as Microsoft PowerPoint or something from your desktop to show off to the attendees. The purpose of this article is to walk you through how screen sharing works in Firefox and some best practices for presentation software.

How to Screen Share

To start screen sharing you will need to either be inside the Green Room or in one of our Lounge/Breakout rooms. When in those rooms you should see a button with this logo and may say "Present".

You should receive a pop-up under the URL bar with the different screen share options for Firefox.

Screen Share Options

You will have 2 options to choose from. Depending on what you are trying to share the behaviors of each option may vary. In both options, you are given the option to mute website notifications while sharing.

  • Screen - This will allow you to select entire desktops. If you have multiple monitors you will be given the option to select either monitor. 
  • Window - This allows you to select an open application window.

Screen Sharing Slide Presentations

Let's go over the most used slide presentation software for Mac. Ideally, it is best to have access to two monitors when screen sharing a presentation. One monitor is to be used for the presentation and the other monitor is to view the green room and chat interaction panel. If you do not have access to a secondary monitor that is ok, we will guide you with some best practices for that.

Microsoft PowerPoint, Keynote, and Google Slides are the most frequently used presentation applications on our platform. While they are similar in function, how you share them can be quite different. On top of that, you will have further differences if you use only one monitor versus two.

Screen Sharing Microsoft PowerPoint

This is the most popular slide presentation software used in presentations. Based on if you have one or more monitors will vary the way you set up the presentation. A two-monitor setup is ideal since it will allow you to see both your slides and the Welcome event in full.

1 Monitor

Video walkthrough here:

Go to "Slide Show", then "Set Up Slide Show".

Choose "Browsed by an individual (window).

While still in "Slide Show", choose "Play from Start" on the left.

Resize the PowerPoint to be about 2/3rds of your screen (might take some trial and error here depending on your screen size, a larger screen is better for this).

Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner).

Choose the "Window or screen" drop-down and select the PowerPoint window. You should see a preview of the selection. You will also be able to mute website notifications on this screen.

From here, resize your two windows (PowerPoint and Welcome) into whatever is comfortable for you. Note that you'll still need to keep the PowerPoint window fairly big as if you try to make it smaller it will resize your presentation within the Welcome stage.

2 Monitors

Video walkthrough here:

Go to "Slide Show", then "Set Up Slide Show".

Choose "Browsed by an individual (window)".

Move the PowerPoint to the secondary screen and maximize the PowerPoint window by clicking the green maximize button on the top left of the window. Then hit "Play from Start" and maximize the window again.

Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner).

Choose the appropriate screen in the drop-down under "Window or screen". You will also be able to mute website notifications on this screen.

The presentation needs to be an active window for you to change slides with the arrow keys. Be sure the presentation is the last thing you clicked on so you can change the slides.

Screen Sharing Keynote

Keynote functions pretty well with Firefox and in some cases (sharing with one monitor) we recommend it just beneath using Chrome and Google slides. 

1 Monitor

Video walkthrough here:

First, please make sure you have the latest update. Otherwise, this might not work as intended.

Go to "Play"> Select "In Window" > "Play Slideshow" Please note that you cannot be in fullscreen in your browser for the Keynote window to show up in this section.

Resize the keynote to be about 2/3rds of your screen (might take some trial and error here depending on your screen size).

Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner).

Choose "Select window or screen" and then find your Keynote presentation and hit "Allow". You will also be able to mute website notifications on this screen.

From here, resize your two windows (Keynote and Welcome) into whatever is comfortable for you. Keynote is much better about resizing the window and keeping the presentation at an appropriate size on the Welcome stage than your other options, so this is not as finicky on one monitor as other presentation tools. In other words, you are able to resize to almost any size screen that you find comfortable and not have resizing issues on the main stage.

2 Monitors

Video walkthrough here:

First, please make sure you have the latest update. Otherwise, this might not work as intended.

Go to "Play"> Select "In Window" > "Play Slideshow".

Move the Keynote to the appropriate screen and maximize the Keynote.

Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner).

Choose the appropriate window or screen in the drop-down menu.

 

Screen Sharing Google Slides

You can screen share Google Slides as an application window but we would advise you to do it from Google Chrome and share it as a Chrome Tab. You can check out our Chrome Guide for more details.

1 Monitor

Video walkthrough here:

Have your Google Slides presentation loaded up in a separate window from Welcome. Get invited and join the green room, then hit the  "present" button on the lower left-hand corner.

Choose the window that has your Google Slides presentation. 

Now go to your Google Slides presentation. Click on the "Slideshow" button. This will cause your presentation to go full screen which we do not want, so on the presentation, go to the bottom left and click the three dots and select "Exit Full Screen".  You can then resize/move the application window to your liking while also being able to see the Welcome platform. You can use arrow keys to change slides and use the Presenter pop-out to control the presentation.

2 Monitors

Video walkthrough here:

Move your Welcome Window and your presentation windows to separate monitor screens. Have your Google slides presentation loaded up by clicking slide show at the top right or in the view tab. 

 

Once you have entered the Green Room in the Welcome window, click the  "present" button on the lower left-hand corner.

Choose the window with your Google Slides presentation in the drop-down and click "Allow". Choosing just a screen to share can result in your slides not advancing correctly so please make sure to select the correct screen for this. You can also choose to deactivate screen notifications from Firefox here.

To change slides you will need to have it as your active window. You will be able to use the arrow key to move slides back and forth.
