Screen Sharing on Chrome Mac OSX

Screen sharing from Chrome on a Mac is our recommended method to screen share for this device type. Screen sharing allows you to either share a slide-based presentation such as Microsoft PowerPoint or something from your desktop to show off to the attendees. The purpose of this article is to walk you through how screen sharing works in Chrome and some best practices for presentation software.

How to Screen Share

To start screen sharing you will need to either be inside the Green Room or in one of our Lounge/Breakout rooms. When in those rooms you should see a button with this logo and may say "Present".

You should receive a pop up with the different screen share options for Chrome

Screen Share Options

You will have 3 options to choose from. Depending on what you are trying to share the behaviors of each option may vary.Â 

  • Entire Screen - This will allow you to select entire desktops. If you have multiple monitors you will be given the option to select either monitor.
  • Window - Allows you to select a specific open application window
  • Chrome Tab - This allows you to select an open tab in Chrome. You are also given the option at the bottom left to toggle on "share audio" for that tab.

Screen Sharing Slide Presentations

Let's go over the most used slide presentation software for Mac. Ideally, it is best to have access to two monitors when screen sharing a presentation. One monitor is to be used for the presentation and the other monitor is to view the green room and chat interaction panel. If you do not have access to a secondary monitor that is ok, we will guide you with some best practices for that.

Microsoft PowerPoint, Keynote, and Google Slides are the most frequently used presentation applications on our platform. While they are similar in function, how you share them can be quite different. On top of that, you will have further differences if you use only one monitor versus two.

Screen Sharing Microsoft PowerPoint

This is the most popular slide presentation software used in presentations. Based on if you have one or more monitors will vary the way you set up the presentation. A two-monitor setup is ideal since it will allow you to see both your slides and the Welcome event in full which allows you to engage with the audience.

1 Monitor

Video walkthrough here:

Within PowerPoint Go to "Slide Show", then "Set Up Slide Show".

Choose "Browsed by an individual (window).

While still in "Slide Show", choose "Play from Start" on the left.

Resize the PowerPoint to be about 2/3rds of your screen (might take some trial and error here depending on your screen size, a larger screen is better for this).

Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner).

Choose "Application Window" and select the PowerPoint window.

From here, resize your two windows (PowerPoint and Welcome) into whatever is comfortable for you. Note that you'll still need to keep the PowerPoint window fairly big as if you try to make it smaller it will resize your presentation within the Welcome stage.

2 Monitors

Video walkthrough here:

Go to "Slide Show", then "Set Up Slide Show".

Choose "Browsed by an individual (window)".

Move the PowerPoint to the secondary screen.

Maximize the PowerPoint window by clicking the green button on the top left of the window.

Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner)

You can either choose the appropriate screen under "Your Entire Screen" or the application window. Just make sure that your PowerPoint is active on the second monitor.

The presentation needs to be an active window for you to change slides with the arrow keys. Be sure the presentation is the last thing you clicked on.

Screen Sharing Keynote

2 Monitors

Video walkthrough here:

First, please make sure you have the latest update. Otherwise, this might not work as intended.

Go to "Play"> Select "In Window" > "Play Slideshow".

Move the Keynote to the appropriate screen and maximize the Keynote.

Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner).

Screen Sharing Google Slides

1 Monitor

Video walkthrough here:

Have your Google Slides presentation loaded up in a separate window from Welcome. Get invited and join the green room, then hit the "present" button on the lower left-hand corner.

Choose "Chrome Tab", then your Google Slides presentation, and hit share. Please note that you can check the "Share audio" check box in the bottom left if you intend to include audio in your presentation (not including speaking through the Welcome platform).

Now go to your Google Slides presentation. You can either go to "view", then "present". Or you can simply press "Slideshow" on the upper right-hand corner. You can then resize/move the application window to your liking while also being able to see the Welcome platform. You can use arrow keys to change slides, just make sure that you are clicked into the slide deck.
