Chat Guide

Chat is the primary way for your attendees and speakers to communicate with each other. Let's dive into what chat channels and features are available.

  • Chat Channels
  • Giphy and Emojis
  • Pinned Chat
  • Chat Reactions

Chat Channels

Public

This is available to every user type and is also the primary channel for attendees to talk. For producers and Speakers this is your primary non-verbal way of engaging the audience. 

Speakers

This is a private chat that only speakers and producers can see. This is the primary way to engage with your speakers outside of the Green Room.

Staff

Private chat only for producers. This is the primary way to engage with other producers outside of the Green Room.

DM

Private direct messages will be organized here. Direct messages can be with any user role. To initiate a DM you will need to hover over the user in the People Tab and click on the DM button to start.

Help

Organized area where users can start a help chat with the producers of the event. It is best to direct users having technical issues to go here rather than troubleshoot in the public chat.

Giphy and Emojis

You can add emojis and even gifs into the chat. Simply clicking emoji button to the right of the text field will create the pop up. You can also type /giphy [text] and it will also create a search for gifs.

Pinned Chats

You can pin important messages to public chat. These will stay at the very top of the chat feed and users can cycle through them. We don't have any limits to how many you can have.

How to add and remove pins

To pin a chat simply hover over the chat text > a pop up will appear > click 3 dots on the far right > select "Pin"

To remove a pin you should see a "Remove Pin" button in white appear at the top with the pinned chat. You can also do the same as you did to create a pin but now you will see unpin

Chat Reactions

Users can react to specific chats with some emoji options. By simply hovering over a chat a pop up will appear with reaction options.

Call to Action (CTA) Guide

Attendee engagement is tricky in a virtual environment. Our Call to Action assets is a solution that you can utilize to keep attendees attention and provide increased value for the attendees in your event. 

What is special about our CTA overlays is that it will create an interactable button on the stage, link in the chat, and even a QR code can be displayed on the stage. Secondarily, any clicks performed on the button and links will be recorded and available in the analytics data.

You can easily find our custom call to action overlays within our Stage Kit.

If you are using the agenda builder, stage kit shows up after you click "Add to Session"

If you are still using our Legacy version of the studio, Stage Kit is available at the bottom left of the screen.

Hubspot Integration Documentation

Overview

Welcome’s Hubspot integration enables our customers to use the actions a Contact takes in Welcome to inform the next step of that Contact’s buyer’s journey.

The integration is streamlined to require minimal configuration. It makes it so your Hubspot Contacts need only click a link to get into an event, without going through registration. It sends back clear, easy-to-interpret signals of attendee sentiment, in real-time, for follow-up action. Some examples of things you can do with the Welcome integration are:

  • Signal an Account Executive in real time when their prospect is attending one of your Webinars, or when a qualified account engages with a “Do you want to talk to a Salesperson?” Poll 

  • Increase the Hubspot lead score by 25 points for any lead which has spent more than 10 minutes at a specific Webinar.

  • Create new Contacts for people who attend your webinars with their custom registration fields 

 

Note to Previous Hubspot Integration Users

If you were using the previous version of our Hubspot integration which leverages the built-in Welcome Properties – Invited Welcome Events, Registered Welcome Events, and Attended Welcome Events – the new integration does NOT update these fields anymore. 

Setup – Connecting Welcome to your Hubspot Instance 

  1. Log in to your Welcome Producer Account at app.experiencewelcome.com
  2. Navigate to Integrations
  3. Click configure
  4. Log in to your Hubspot Instance in the window that pops up. 
    1. Enter any name for your authentication and Click Create. Leave the Developer API Key field blank.
    2. Choose the Hubspot account you want to connect to.
    3. Confirm with Hubspot that Welcome should connect to your account.
    4. Click Finish
  5. Next, you will be asked to Map Welcome Fields to Hubspot Fields. If you are okay with the default field mapping configuration You can just click save.
  6. You can always update your Hubspot Authentication or field mappings by clicking Edit and selecting which settings you want to update from the dropdown.
  7. You can remove the connection to Hubspot and your field mapping configuration by clicking Reset. This will not delete any data. You can always connect to Hubspot and set a new field mapping configuration again.

 

Setup – Mapping Welcome Fields to Hubspot Contact Fields

Note: if you were using the previous version of our Hubspot integration which leverages the built-in Welcome Properties – Invited Welcome Events, Registered Welcome Events, and Attended Welcome Events – do not map data to these fields.

  2. All Welcome fields that will map to Hubspot Contacts are visible on this page. The integration will send other Welcome data to Hubspot in the form of timeline activities on Contact objects. (See the “What data gets synced and when” section below). 
  3. Not all Field mappings are configurable by you. The destination of those mappings that you cannot update are in black boxes. The destination of those mappings that you can update are in grey boxes.

  5. You must also select the Update Policy for a field. You can choose from: “Always Update”, “Update If Empty”, and “Don’t Update”.
  6. Custom Registration Fields:

    2. For example, if the custom field is "City", the integration will sync the attendee's answer: "New York"

    4. Mapping custom fields is optional.

How do I send registrants into Welcome from Hubspot

To send Contacts into Welcome such that they don’t need to register again, we recommend using our Quick Registration link functionality. 

 

Alternatively, you can create a Hubspot workflow to send registrants into Welcome, populate a Unique Link Property on each registrant’s Contact Object, and use that property your pre-event communications. To do so, do the following:

    1. Navigate to your Organization Integration settings.
    2. Click Edit Field Mapping
    3. Ensure that you have selected a mapping for a registrants’ Unique Links.
    4. Otherwise, create a Contact property in Hubspot for the Unique Link and select that property in the field mapper
    5. Navigate back to your integrations settings and Copy the webhook url listed
    6. In Hubspot create a contact based Workflow with your event’s Hubspot registration form as the trigger.
    7. Add a step to Send a Webhook
    8. Choose the POST Webhook option
    9. Paste the URL you copied from the Welcome Event Dashboard into the Webhook URL field
    10. Navigate to the Welcome event that you want to send registrants to and copy the Event ID of that event. (It’s the 6 characters in the event link after app.experiencewelcome.com/events/) 
    11. Replace the “ReplaceWithYourYourEventId” String in the Webhook URL with the actual Welcome event ID. In this case “wnuK0Z”
    12. Click Save and Publish your workflow
    13. Now when someone fills out your Hubspot registration form they will appear in the attendees list of your Welcome event
    14. Their Contact object will also contain their unique link to get into the Welcome event. This link will be stored in the Contact property you set for the Attendee’s Unique Link in the Field Mapping Configuration step above.
    15. You can use the Unique Link Property in your Hubspot emails as a personalization token.

Unable to Screen Share

Screen sharing in the virtual space is extremely important and very common. It also happens to be one of the most common troubleshooting issues with web based platforms. This issue is most commonly associated to Mac users. If a Mac user has never screen shared with a web browser before. The device system settings most likely hasn't given the browser app permissions to do so. Fortunately, this is an easy fix.

What does the error look like

How you'll know if you are getting blocked by the system while in Welcome. You will receive a red pop up at the top right corner of the screen.

How do we fix this

Let's walkthrough how to enable these permissions. This is most commonly associated with Mac devices. Windows devices very rarely have this permission blocked out of the box. 

An important thing to note is that some updates to your browser or system can inadvertently reset permissions even if it looks like the box has already been checked. In these cases, please remove the permissions, re-add them, and then restart your browser. 

Mac System Settings

  1. Go to Security and Privacy settings (Press CMD+ Space bar and type Security)
  2. Click the Privacy tab on the right
  3. On the left side bar scroll until you find Screen Recording
  4. Check the box for Google Chrome on the right for each section
    1. You may need to use the padlock at the bottom left to allow checking the box
  5. You will be prompted to restart Chrome if it is currently open
  6. Go back to the event and jump in

Windows System Settings

Windows does not have a system setting for this specifically. If you are getting blocked it is most likely from some sort of security app denying the browser from screen sharing. You will either need to disable the app, allow the permission from within the app, and/or contact your IT team that manages permissions for you.

  1. Log in to your Welcome Producer Account at app.experiencewelcome.com
  2. Navigate to Integrations at the left side of the screen
  3. Click configure and click New Authentication in the popup that comes up
  4. Log in to your Hubspot Instance in the window that pops up. 
    1. Enter any name for your authentication.

    2. In the API endpoint domain field, provide your Marketo REST API endpoint identified in the image below.

      1. This can be found by navigating to the Admin tab in Marketo

    3. Get the values for the Client ID and Client Secret Fields
      1. Open the Launchpoint dropdown menu in Marketo
      2. Select View Details on an Installed Service you have running
      3. You will see the Client ID and Client Secret fields
    4. When you have the right values pasted in, click Create
    5. Click Finish

  5. Map Welcome Fields to Marketo Fields

    1. First, you must set a Marketo Template Program Id.
      1. To get this ID, navigate in Marketo to the program you want to set as a template. This integration duplicates your template program for every Welcome event you create so that each Welcome Event is associated with a Marketo Program.
      2. A Marketo Program's ID can be found at the end of its URL, after the ME and before the A. See the portion of the URL in the red box below for an example
    2. After setting a Template Program Id, you need to map Welcome fields to Marketo Lead and Program Fields. See the Mapping Welcome Fields to Marketo Fields Section Below for more information.

  3. All Welcome fields that will map to Marketo Leads and Program Members are visible in the Field Mapping dialog. Data that is not mapped in this dialog will not be persisted to Marketo.
  4. Not all Field mappings are configurable by you. The destination of those mappings that you cannot update are in black boxes. The destination of those mappings that you can update are in grey boxes.
  5. When you click a mappable field to update you will be taken to a page to search for the field in Marketo that you can map to.

    2. Email, First Name, Last Name are not mappable fields and will always be updated by the integration
    3. Role, Company, and Unique link are mappable fields. You can provide attendees their Unique Link in your pre-event communications from Marketo to allow them to bypass registration on Welcome.
  8. Custom Fields
    1. Custom Registration Fields are populated in the target Marketo Program whenever someone new completes their registration on Welcome.

  9. Event Registration Analytics

    2. These fields can be mapped to Custom Program Member Fields in your Target Marketo Program so you can act on attendee analytics data.
  10. Attendee interactions with on-stage CTAs, Attendees Questions in the Q&A tab, and Attendee Poll Responses are also mapped back to your Target Marketo Program. These interactions are sent to your target Marketo program in real time so you can take action on them.

Welcome Features

We have many features that bring value and elevate the experience from just a stage on the screen to something jaw-dropping. 

Breakout Rooms and Lounges

We have two secondary spaces to drive engagement with the attendees in a much smaller intimate space. Breakouts are our small private groups that are curated by the producer. Attendees will have to be invited by the producer to join these groups. Lounges are more of a public small group that any attendee can jump in and out of at anytime the room is open. We have detailed guides for each Breakout Room Guide and Lounge Guide. Please read both for specifics.

Breakout rooms vs Lounges

  • Both can have between 2-16 people but, Lounges have spaces we call Watchers that allow attendees to jump into the room and not be on camera. Watchers are still able to chat with the other participants in the Lounge.
  • Users will need their camera and mic on and working to be able to use them (not applicable to Watchers in Lounges).
  • Both options have the capability to enable photo booths.
  • From the attendee's perspective, when inside the rooms they look identical. As a producer, the administration panels for each are different.

Q&A

Our Q&A feature is one of our attendee engagement tools that allow attendees to ask questions directly to the speakers publicly. These questions can be up-voted or down-voted by other attendees to give the producer a sense of what is the most popular. Check out our detailed Q&A Guide for specifics.

Polls

This allows the producer and speakers to create instant engagement with the attendees. Polls can be voted on via the Poll tab in the event interaction panel or the producer can push the poll to the stage and attendees can vote by clicking the poll answer on the stage. Check out our detailed Polls Guide for specifics.

Video on Demand

Our Video on Demand feature allows you to share recordings of your event once it has ended. You can set it to allow anyone to view the event recording or you can have it set to private by requiring registration. Check out our detailed Video On Demand Guide for specifics.

Streaming Out

We allow RTMP out to up to 5 different destinations. We currently support Youtube, Twitch, Facebook, LinkedIn, and allow you to enter custom RTMP out details. Check out our detailed Streaming Out - General Guide for specifics.

Raise Hands

Raise Hands is a feature that will allow attendees and speakers to notify the producer that they have a question they want to share live on stage. This allows producers to find individuals and bring them into the Green Room and then onto the stage. Check out our detailed Raise Hands Guide for specifics.

Accessibility for your event is essential, and for many organizations, closed captioning may be a requirement. Best Practices

Setting up your hardware

  • Use the compatibility checker before logging in to identify any possible issues: https://app.experiencewelcome.com/compatibility-check
  • We strongly recommend using Chrome for all speakers. Please ensure you have your browser permissions for video and microphone allowed; if you plan on screen sharing on a Mac please make sure your system preferences are set correctly (detailed below). You can also use Firefox, Safari, or Edge if you want, but we recommend Chrome.
    • If on a Mac, you will need to check privacy/security settings to allow screen recording for Chrome.
      • Go to System Preferences >Security & Privacy > Scroll down to Screen Recording > Check box for Google Chrome.
    • Our full permissions troubleshooting article is available for further details.
  • Do not share your unique link with anyone else. If some one does use your unique link it will kick you out of the event due to only 1 unique user can be online with your profile.
  • If you are screen sharing a 4k display may need to scale your resolution down to 1080p for the event.
  • Make sure your internet connection is good and stable; we recommend at least 50Mbps download and 25Mbps upload.
  • Make sure your laptop is plugged in and in a charging state.
  • Close all of your non-essential programs and limit the number of browser tabs you have open; we recommend just having the Welcome tab open if possible.
  • Turn off your auto-updating apps, notifications on your device.

Setting up your environment

  • Let your colleagues (or your kids and your dog) know that you are presenting live to 5000+ people and best not disturb you. Maybe lock the door! ;-)
  • If you are going to be screen sharing, please set up your slides on a second monitor if possible as this will allow you to see both Welcome and your presentation.
    • Check out this article if you need more help with screen sharing.
  • Make sure to hide anything personal or sensitive on any screen, tab, desktop, bookmarks bar, and turn off notifications. Lets not forget to silence your mobile devices.
  • Remember that you will not have the option of using a virtual background unless you use a 3rd party app (Snapcam or mmhmm for instance). You may want to put in some time and energy to make your background look amazing!

You will have two audio sliders that you can adjust for your needs.

Recording Events

Best Practices

  • Starting a recording takes a few seconds (about 10-15 seconds). It's best to start recording a little bit earlier than the event's start to ensure you capture everything (i.e. don't start the recording just as you push a speaker to the stage). You can always cut extra footage during the editing process.
  • You can start and stop the recording as many times as you'd like. It will create a new file each time.
  • You cannot record breakout rooms and lounges.
  • If your event is over three hours, the best practice is to start and stop the recordings during intermissions to create new video files in the system. Extremely long videos (3+ hours) are prone to corruption/error and will not generate an SRT file (captions).
  • Double-check to make sure that you've hit "stop record" at the end of your event. This is very important. If you forget to do this the recording might become corrupted/unplayable.
  • If you need to change your event time to enable recordings, please make sure you set the time at least 15 minutes in the future to allow the recording server to spin up.

How To Record

In the Control Room above and to the right of the main stage will be the record button in red

    • The recording button will appear approximately one hour before the start time of your event. Otherwise, it is greyed out.

Where To Find Recordings

Recordings are stored in the Event Dashboard.

    • Go to the left sidebar and toward the bottom is the "Recording" section. Within there, click on the "Download Recording" button to download the video file.
      • Please note that recordings never expire. You will have access to them as long as you have access to Welcome.

Browser

Refreshing your Browser

Browser Permissions

  • The most common issues with mics and audio are the privacy/security permissions within the browser settings.
  • Programs conflicting with each other can also throw an error. To ensure that your speaker has a smooth onboarding, please make sure that their mic/camera is not being used by other programs (Zoom, Skype, Hangouts, etc).
    • Make sure to turn these off when joining a Welcome event. If you're not sure what could be causing the conflict, give your computer a hard restart.

Connection

Turn off VPNs and firewalls if possible

  • If you are seeing a black/blank screen on the Welcome Platform it is most likely due to a VPN, please turn that off if possible.
    • We see these turned on mostly for work computers and/or work wifi. Have your speakers try to use a personal computer on a home wifi network. In some extreme cases, you may want to suggest turning a mobile device into a wifi hotspot.
  • Adblocking extensions can also cause issues with Welcome. We recommend turning those off or joining through an incognito/private window.
  • In some rare cases, restarting the modem/router has fixed this issue.

Good internet speeds - at least 50mbps download and 25mbps upload

  • We strongly recommend at least 50mbps download and 25mbps upload. If speeds are lower, your speakers will potentially experience choppy/laggy visuals and sound. It is best to have good speed on a solid router and use a hardwired connection whenever possible.
  • To conserve bandwidth, make sure your speakers should try to minimize the number of devices using their internet while they are presenting (i.e. smart TVs, mobile devices, etc.)

Troubleshooting Common Speaker Issues

Ensuring that your event goes smoothly requires your speakers to have no issues. Below we have listed some common issues that a speaker might run into:

  • Registration or log-in issues.
  • Speaker can't join the Green Room and/or lounges/breakouts.
  • Speaker can't hear anyone in the Green Room. Other people can't hear the speaker in the Green Room.
  • Speaker is having internet issues (audio is lagging, video is cutting in and out).
  • Speaker is seeing a blank screen despite there being a presentation going on.
  • Speaker keeps getting “kicked out”.
  • Speaker’s audio is giving a lot of feedback.
  • Speaker can’t screen share.
  • When screen sharing, the speaker’s presentation takes up the whole screen, and can’t see Welcome.
  • Speaker's screen share is pixelated.

 

Registration or login issues

  • VPNs, firewalls, adblocking extensions, or other security can sometimes block Welcome. If it's a VPN or firewall issue please ask your speaker to resolve it on their side (turning it off, switching computers, etc.).
    • Welcome Support can also toggle on something on our end to mitigate these issues. Please contact support@experiencewelcome.com if you are not able to resolve these issues by deactivating security software/programs.
  • If your speakers are having trouble logging you in you can send them a "one-click registration" email from the Event Dashboard. Just make sure they check their spam/junk folders for the email, or copy the link from the dashboard and send it to them. From there they will just need to fill out a few details and they can enter the event right after.

  • Microphone/camera permissions issue within the browser (most common). On Macs, there are also system-level permissions that can also cause issues in some cases.
  • A conflict with the microphone/camera being used by another program (Zoom, Skype, Hangouts, etc).
    • The best practice is to make sure to turn these off. If you're not sure what else is accessing your microphone/camera, give your computer a restart.

Speaker can't hear anyone in the Green Room. Other people can't hear the speaker in the Green Room

  • Have them unmute the Green Room and mainstage. Please also have them confirm their Green Room audio is not muted.
  • Have them check AV settings by clicking on the A/V Gear icon in the Green Room. From here, they should be able to swap between different microphone options.
  • Have them close any browsers or applications (i.e. Zoom) that could be using their mic and/or camera.
  • Have them try logging in using an incognito/private window.
  • Check computer sound settings:
    • For Mac devices:
      • Go to System Preferences
      • Choose Sound
      • Select the output and input they would like to use
      • Have them refresh their browser and log back into Welcome
    • For PC devices:
      • Go to Settings
      • Choose Sound
      • Select the output and input they would like to use
      • Have them refresh their browser and log back into Welcome
  • If none of the above works try a hard computer restart.

Speaker is having internet issues (audio is lagging, video is cutting in and out, etc.)

  • Hopefully, you've confirmed that the speaker has a decent internet connection before they join. We recommend having speakers run through our Compatability Checker prior to the event.
  • Some solutions to fix connection issues: 
    • Restart computer
    • Restart router
    • After the above there isn't a ton you can do from your side. You can ask them to maybe conserve as much local bandwidth (have other people on the same wifi log off, close uneccessary tabs/programs, etc.) as possible until their presentation is over.
  • Producers also can instruct people in the Green Room to turn off video to conserve bandwidth.

Speaker is seeing a blank screen despite there being a presentation going on

  • This is almost always related to device security or a connection issue. For device security, please ask your speaker to deactivate from the security service. For internet issues, please follow the steps in the above section.

Speaker keeps getting “kicked out”

  • This usually means the person has another window or tab with Welcome open. Have them close everything except the one instance of Welcome they are in. Please also make sure that your speaker does not share their account details or one-click link with anyone else.

Speaker’s audio is giving a lot of feedback

  • Mute everyone in the Green Room except the speaker to see if it continues. If it does, it's something causing feedback on the speaker's end (poor connection of mic, dying bluetooth headset, etc.). If it doesn’t it's someone else in the Green Room, make sure no one happens to have another computer with the Welcome event running in the background where the noise might bleed in.
  • Ask the speaker to use headphones or lower the volume of their speakers.

Speaker can’t screenshare

  • Most screen sharing issues happen when a speaker is using Firefox or Edge. We recommend that anyone screen sharing uses Chrome.
  • If on a Mac, you will need to check privacy/security settings to allow screen recording for Chrome.
    • Go to System Preferences >Security & Privacy > Scroll down to Screen Recording > Check box for Google Chrome.

Speaker's screen sharing is pixelated

  • The producer might need to “kick the screen share” out of the Green Room and have the speaker re-share it.

Breakout Room Guide

Breakouts allow for magic moments to occur in a much more intimate environment. Breakouts are smaller personalized sessions with up to 16 attendees to engage and discuss with each other. In an event, you can have up to 50 breakout rooms per stage. Our breakout rooms allow events to become more engaging and, for lack of a better word, eventful.

Let's break down how to set up Breakouts to create these magic moments.

Table of Contents

  • Enabling Breakouts
  • Creating Breakout Rooms
  • Navigating and Editing Breakout Rooms
    • Top Panel - Administrative Panel
    • Center Panel - Breakout Rooms View
    • Left Panel - Stage and Green Room
    • Right Panel - People Panel

Enabling Breakouts

  • Go to Event Details in your Event Dashboard.
  • Scroll to Event Customization, and at the bottom, check the box for Breakouts and click Save.
    • You can change the display name for breakouts at the top of the Event Customization.

Creating Breakout Rooms

  • At the top bar of the Control Room, click on Breakouts.
  • To the right, click on New Session.
  • Add Session Name.
    • Session Name will only be visible to producers and staff.
  • Breakout Assignment - You have 3 choices "Auto-Assign", "Manual Assign", and "CSV".
    • Auto-Assign - will automatically generate rooms and assign them based on your attendees per room slider. You are able to choose if you want to include participants that are online at the time or to include offline participants.
      • Speakers DO NOT get auto-assigned.
    • Manual Assign - will create enough rooms for the number of total attendees. The producer will need to manually move attendees into the rooms.
    • CSV - Will create rooms and assign attendees based on the CSV. Please make sure to use our CSV format when creating rooms via CSV.
  • Adjust Duration based on your needs.
  • Add a Topic, this is optional but having one can help drive an initial discussion.
  • When everything is filled out properly click "Next" to generate the rooms.

Navigating and Editing Breakout Rooms

Top Panel - Administrative Panel

  • Send Announcement
      • Sending an announcement will allow global communication to all attendees. This will be in the form of a pop-up at the top of the user's screen.

  • Edit 
      • Session Name - allows changing the name of the session.
      • Upload new CSV - Allows uploading a CSV of the room arrangements after the fact. This is not available after you open the doors for Breakouts.
      • Duration - Adjust room duration. This is not available after you open the doors for Breakouts, but you are able to add time to the bottom left of the screen.

      • Add More Time

  5. Anonymous Q&A - All - All questions submitted will be anonymous
  6. Raise Hand - Attendees can raise their hand, letting the producer know they would like to go on stage. They will be prompted to confirm their A/V settings, then be on standby for the producer to invite them into the Green Room.

    • Reject - Permanently removes the question

  • Unanswered Queue
    • Add/Remove to Stage - Moves the question to and from the stage. When you remove it from the stage it will move the question to the Answered Queue
    • Mark as Answered - Reverts the question back to Answered
    • Invite to Green Room - Sends a Green Room invite to the attendee that submitted the question. If the attendee accepts you can send the attendee to the stage and ask the question live!

    • Add/Remove to Stage - Moves the question to and from the stage. 
    • Mark as Unanswered - Reverts the question back to the Unanswered queue
    • Delete - Permanently removes the question

Troubleshooting Camera and Microphone Permissions

This is a common view when joining a room that requires a camera and microphone but your permissions are blocking access to the devices.

This is an easy fix and we have 3 different ways of resolving this. This article is going to break it down step by step. This guide will be using Chrome as the browser since it is our recommended browser to use with Welcome.

Approving Permission by Popup

By default, Chrome has Camera and Microphone permissions set to Ask. What this will do is cause a pop-up to appear at the top left of the screen just under the URL bar. Please do not ignore and close the pop-up, it is asking to approve permissions. Simply click Allow and then in Welcome the Camera and Microphone settings become available. In some cases, you may need to refresh the browser.

Approving Permissions via lock by URL bar

If the pop-up does not appear or if you closed the pop-up by accident. Have no fear we can still make changes to approve permissions by clicking the lock icon in the URL bar.

  1. On the URL Address Bar at the top, all the way to the left you should see a lock or padlock
  2. Click the lock and a pop-up will appear
  3. In the pop-up you should see permission drop downs for both camera and microphone
  4. Change settings to Allow
  5. Refresh browser
  6. Jump back into the event

Approving Permissions by Site Settings

If all of the above does not work or appear, the last thing we can do is go into Site Settings.

  1. On the URL Address Bar at the top, all the way to the left you should see a lock or padlock
  2. Click the lock and a pop up will appear
  3. In the pop up you should see Site Settings
  4. This should take you to a new tab and you should see permission drop downs for both camera and microphone

  1. Change settings to Allow
  2. Refresh browser
  3. Jump back into the event

System Setting Permissions

If you are going to be utilizing your camera/microphone and/or screen sharing on a Mac for the first time you might need to update these settings in your System Preferences. Without having these settings properly set, you will receive a permission error stating "Permission denied by system." Below is a quick walkthrough on how you can enable these permissions. Windows devices tend to have fewer of these situations but we also provide some steps for Windows devices. 

An important thing to note is that some updates to your browser can cause your computer not to recognize permissions you have already set. In cases like these, please remove the permissions, re-add them, and then restart your browser.

Mac System Settings

  1. Go to Security and Privacy settings (Press CMD+ Space bar and type Security)
  2. Click the Privacy tab on the right
  3. On the left side bar scroll until you find and select Camera, Microphone, and/or Screen Recording
  4. Check the box for Google Chrome on the right for each section
  5. You will be prompted to restart Chrome if it is currently open
  6. Go back to the event and jump in

Windows System Settings

  1. Go to Privacy settings (Press Windows and type Privacy)
  2. On the left side bar scroll until you find and select Camera and/or Microphone
  3. Scroll down and check that "Allow Access to the Camera/Microphone on this device" is ON and toggles are enabled for Apps and Desktop Apps
  4. Go back to the event and jump in

People - Attendees, Speakers, and Staff

This is where you manage the users of your event. If needed, you can change the name of these in "Event Details". Anyone except staff who registers will default as an Attendee. Speakers will either need to be added manually or promoted from attendee to speaker after they have registered. The interface for each of the user types are largely the same with minor differences. Let's dive into the different functions and note the differences as we go.

Sending Invites

You have multiple ways to send invites from this section ranging from bulk groups to individual users.

Sending invites in bulk can simply be done by clicking the "Send Invites" button at the top right corner. This will send invites to all those with an unconfirmed status. This feature is unavailable for Staff.

If you have a group of specific users you would like to send to you can do so by checking the boxes next to their names then clicking "Send Invite(s)"

Lastly, you can either send a One-Click Link to a user via email or copy and paste it to the user with whatever method you would like to do it. To do so you will see an Action Column to the right of every user.

Clicking on the 3 stacked dots at the far right will reveal a drop-down. Select One Click Link options that fit best for you. One-Click Links will allow users to jump straight into events with the least amount of registration friction. You can check out our One-Click Registration Link Guide for further details.

Managing Users

This is where you can add, modify, and remove users. Along with altering user permission roles.

Adding User(s)

Adding a user or users can be done in one of two ways. Either manually one by one or in bulk via a CSV upload.

Adding an individual user is pretty straightforward. You can click the "Add" button at the top right and a pop-up will appear for you to fill out. The required fields for creating an account are first name, last name, and email address; you do not need to add in a user's role/company or image to create an account. You will not be able to add anyone for Staff; instead, Staff users can simply login to the event and they will automatically populate.

Adding users in bulk can be done with a CSV upload by clicking the "Upload" button at the top right to access the pop-up. We have a specific template and instructions for the uploads that you will need to use (the only required fields are first name, last name, and email address). You can review our CSV Uploads (Attendees/Speakers) article regarding the details. 

You are also able to download a CSV of the users that you have in your people list by clicking the download cloud button all the way to the right. You are able to download up to 25k entries.

It will contain these columns:

  • First Name
  • Last Name
  • Email
  • Role
  • Company
  • Confirmed?
  • Attended?
  • Registered At
  • One-Click Registration Link
  • Any custom registration fields for the event

Modifying User(s)

You can modify two key areas, one being the user profile information and the second being able to promote user roles from attendee to speaker and back.

  • Editing user profiles can be done by clicking the 3 stacked dots in the action column to the far right. A drop-down will appear and an "Edit" option will be available. You will only be able to edit name, role, and company fields. If you have custom fields as part of your registration they will not be editable. 
  • Promoting an attendee to a speaker can be done by clicking the 3 stacked dots in the action column to the far right of the users row. A drop down will appear and you will be able to select "convert to speaker". The same can do the same for speakers to swap them to an attendee. This function is not available for Staff.

Raise Hands is a feature that will allow attendees and speakers to notify the producer that they have a question.

 

Table of Contents

  • How Attendees and Speakers Use this Feature
  • Producers View for Raise Hands

How to Enable the Feature

  1. Go to the cogwheel in the right side bar
  2. Click the Raise Hands toggle

How Attendees and Speakers Use this Feature

Attendees will see this in the Q&A portion of the right side bar at the bottom

Once they click on Raise Hands the user will be prompted with a microphone and camera check just like if you were going into a green room. After the mic/camera check they will be informed that the producer has been notified and invite will be sent to the user when ready.

The Raise Hand button will now change to Lower Hand, if the producer denies the users request it will turn back to Raise Hand.

Producers View for Raise Hands

Producers will manage Raise Hands in the People tab of the right side bar. Something to note, you will see a display for how long the users Raise Hand request has been.

You will have 4 options when you hover over the person

  • Left Arrow - Send the person and invite into the Green Room
  • Text Bubbles - This is to send the person a Direct Message
  • 3 Stacked Dots - This will give you an option to Ban the user (This is permanent) 
  • Circle with a Line - This will deny or dismiss the request

Pre-Recording Content on Welcome

The Welcome platform can be used as a production studio to create pre-recorded content for your event. Leveraging recorded content interspersed throughout your event can allow flexibility and reduce the amount of labor that the producer of the event has to manage.

Possible use cases:

  • Housekeeping videos
  • Intro/outro videos
  • Speakers that are unable to attend that day
  • Segments that are not asking for or need engagement from the attendees

  1. Create your event for your recording session in your Organization Dashboard.
    • In some cases, you may want to create multiple recording event sessions that span different dates.
      • Please make sure that your event times are set correctly as well otherwise the recording server will not be spun up. If you need to change the event time, please make sure that you allow a fifteen-minute grace period before you begin to allow the server to spin up.
    • If you are going to re-use the same event please ensure the start and end times do not span for multiple days. We advise keeping everything one day at a time and updating the event times for each new day. This will ensure the recording server is up to date and does not time out.
  2. Add the speaker(s) you need for your recording session within the speaker tab of your Event Dashboard.
  3. Follow the same speaker onboarding process as you would for an event. We have a Speaker Onboarding guide here in case you need it.
    • If the recording session is going to be with one person or a small group, you may be able to get away with onboarding the speaker the same day as the recording.
  4. Day of the recording, perform your tech checks with the speaker(s). If you are having camera or screen sharing permission issues, please refer to our Troubleshooting Permission Issues article.

Best Practices

  • You can start and stop the recording as many times as you'd like. It will create a new file each time. 
  • The recording function does have a 10 to 15 seconds delay after you press start. It's better to press record early. You can always cut extra footage during the editing process.
  • As opposed to live events, recording content allows you to make extra takes. Keep that in mind, and don't always feel forced to do it all in one take.
  • When doing extra takes, make sure the speaker takes a brief pause between them to allow an easier time in editing. 
  • Use overlays and assets; this allows you to create an edge over just a camera recording. We have a couple of articles on using Keynote and Google Slides to create overlays. 

How To Record

In the Control Room above and to the right of the main stage will be the record button.

    • The recording button will appear approximately 1 hour before the start time of your event, otherwise, it is greyed out.
    • If you need to update your event start time, make sure to do it 15 minutes before you intend to start recording.

Where To Find Recordings

Recordings are stored in the Event Dashboard. The recordings will be 720p at 60fps H264 codec MP4 files.

    • Go to the left sidebar, and toward the bottom is the "Recording" section. Within there, click on the "Download Recording" button to download the video file.

Editing Your Videos

If you are going to edit your videos here is a list of things to be mindful about:

  • Audio levels - it's always good to double-check your audio levels to ensure that they are not too quiet or too loud. You don't want audio to go over 0db, this will cause your audio to be too loud and start clipping. A good rule of thumb is to look for an overall mix level of -10db to -14db.
  • Export settings - If you want to use the edited video on Welcome we have some recommendations
    • File Type - MP4
    • Resolution - 720p to 1080p
    • Frame Rate - Use the same rate as the OG file, 60fps

If you are interested in this feature for your organization, please contact your Customer Success Manager for specifics.   2. Click the toggle for Stage Captioning.

How do Attendees turn on Closed Captioning

How to get Closed Caption Transcripts

Another great feature function is that you can pull transcripts for your recordings after your event. To access your transcript, go to your Event Dashboard Recording Tab, and under your recording will be a button to Download Subtitles.

Video On Demand Guide

So you have finished your jaw dropping event and now you want to share it so your attendees can relive it. This feature will do just that, it will give your attendees access to you event recordings. This feature is not only for those that attended the event. Depending on the permissions you have set, you may allow people who did not attend the event to access the recordings.

Enabling Event Recordings

You can find the settings to enable this feature under the Event Details of your Event Dashboard.

  • Toggle "Make Recordings Available" to enable the feature. It will turn on the enable function for the videos in the event recordings page. (Defaults to Off)
    • Auto Publish recordings - Enables Auto publishing, helps remove friction for cases where friction isn't needed. Internal events like all hands is a good example of where you would want it published as soon as its ready.
    • Require registration to view - Can either require users needing to go through the registration process or access them anonymously. Additionally, if your event is set to Email Allow List then only users in your attendee list will be able to access the videos.

Publishing Videos

To publish videos, go to the Recording section of your Event Dashboard. Under every video you will see a toggle to Publish Recording. The Videos are sorted alphabetically and you are able to rename the files.

Accessing the Recordings as an Attendee

We made it very simple to access these recordings as an attendee. The process is exactly the same as going into a live event. The attendee will just need to go to the event link and it will take them to a slightly different view of the event login. 

Uploading Videos

Your recordings are raw files and you may want to edit your videos and upload the edited videos back up. We can help you with that. In the dashboard you will see an "Add" button at the top right corner. Clicking that will create a popup for you to select the video you want to upload.

Splash / Welcome Integration Overview

Welcome offers an integration that connects a Splash Event to a Welcome Event, syncing registrant data between the platforms. 

The integration makes it easier for registrants who sign up on a Splash Registration page to navigate and check in to a Welcome virtual event, with the intent to maximize attendance at your Welcome event.

The following steps will allow a Splash administrator to send Event registrants into the Welcome platform. After a Splash guest is sent to Welcome, the guest’s information in Splash is updated with a unique event link for them to access the event seamlessly. The integration will create new Splash Guests if there are people who checked in to the Welcome Event but were not in Splash.

Help, my Attendees/Speakers can only see a black screen and no audio!

One of the most common issues you will hear from users is "Has it started yet? I only see a black screen and no audio". This is most likely due to either a VPN, some sort of network security like a firewall, and/or a security extension installed in their browser. 

Of the three ways this could happen, a VPN is the most common. The simplest solution to this is to ask if they are able to disable the VPN. This may not be possible in all cases, so let us break down the different ways this can happen with possible solutions.

 

VPN 

  • Turn off VPN if possible.
  • Try a different browser or an incognito/private window.
  • Use a different device; generally, we recommend a personal device as these are less likely to have security installed on the device.
  • View the event on mobile. Attendees will still be able to utilize the chat, Q&A, and polls on mobile. However, they will not be able to join lounges or breakout.
  • If you are recording the event, offer a way to view the event recording.

Firewall

  • Move to a personal network such as using a mobile phone as a wifi hotspot. Sometimes a Guest network will work.
  • Offer your IT team our Networking — troubleshooting & tuning for optimal performance guide.
  • View the event on mobile using a mobile data plan. Mobile allows for all of Welcome's functionality except for joining breakouts or lounges.
  • If you are recording the event, offer a way to view the event recording.

Browser Security

  • Use an incognito/private window of the browser. This will generally disable installed extensions that could be blocking the feed.
  • Manually disable the security extension that was either installed by IT security or even pop-up blockers.

Networking — troubleshooting & tuning for optimal performance

If the audio and video streams for an event are coming through very slowly or cutting in and out, you are likely accessing our application behind a network security filter that is blocking or throttling connections to our media backend.

Network security filters could be running on: your device, a corporate firewall, your wifi access point, or a VPN you are using.

If you can, turning off your VPN before joining the event will generally improve the quality and reliability of the audio and video streams.

Our networking layer has been designed to support the strictest filtering rules we've encountered on our enterprise customers' corporate networks. Specifically, these are:

  • Block all UDP traffic except port 53 (DNS)
  • Block all TCP traffic except for ports 80 and 443 (HTTP and HTTPS respectively)
  • Network Address Translation policy is set to "symmetric"

Under those conditions, our networking layer will establish a TCP connection to a media proxy server running on port 443 (HTTPS), then tunnel all audio and video data through that connection.

If you are a network administrator looking to optimize the event's experience for your users and reduce the bandwidth usage on your internet link, we recommend that you modify your firewall configuration to include the following rules:

allow traffic on:
  UDP port 3478
  TCP and UDP ports 4700 - 5000
  TCP ports 3433, 5668, 5669, 6080, 6443, 8667, 9667, 30011 - 30013
if the destination IP matches a DNS record in one of these domains:
  agora.io edge.agora.io sd-rtn.com edge.sd-rtn.com

Integrations - Welcome into Salesforce

Overview

This article details instructions on how to implement an integration between Salesforce and Welcome. Specifically, this guide will go over how to configure your Salesforce instance to receive data from Welcome. If you are looking for information on sending data from Salesforce to Welcome, please click here.

The Welcome platform will create a new Campaign in Salesforce that is associated with the Event if one does not already exist. The integration will also create new Campaign Members within the Campaign.

Welcome will also send unique, one-click URLs for each Campaign Member in Salesforce. This can be included in email campaigns sent to registrants to click and jump into an event seamlessly. If Pardot is being leveraged with Salesforce, this guide will also go over how to send the one-click URLs back into Pardot to embed in post-registration email communication.

Lastly, the integration will check for updated attendee information every few hours and update all Campaign Members with their most up-to-date registration status at that time.

 

User Permissions

A "Producer" role in Welcome

A Salesforce Profile that has permission to read, write, and edit data to custom fields 

A Pardot Administrator (optional)

 

Configuring Salesforce to Receive Data from Welcome

  • Create a custom field under the "Campaign Member" object in Salesforce. Name the field "Unique Link for Welcome Event". Please note that this must be the exact syntax.
  • Create a custom field under the "Campaign" object in Salesforce. Name the field "Welcome Event Id". Please note that this must be the exact syntax.
  • Provide your Welcome Customer Success Manager or Sales Engineer the following:
    • Your company's Salesforce login URL
    • Salesforce username and password (via a secure method)
  • Navigate to your Welcome Event Dashboard and click on the Integrations tab. From there, click on the "Trigger Integration" button.

Optional: Sending Unique Links from Salesforce to Pardot

  • Create a custom field under the "Lead" object in Salesforce. Name the field "Unique Link for Welcome Event".
  • Utilize Process Builder to replicate the value in the Campaign Member object to the Lead Object's custom field, "Unique Link for Welcome Event".
  • Create a custom field under Prospect in Pardot and name it "Unique Link for Welcome Event". Link this field with the Lead object's "Unique Link for Welcome Event" field. Ensure that the sync behavior has "Use Salesforce's Value" selected.

I Hear Audio Echoing

Generally, if you are hearing echo, it means that there is a device that is channeling your audio back. This can be very distracting for your audience and finding a quick solution will help your event be successful. If you hear an audio echo or audio feedback through your speakers, there are a few possible causes and solutions:

  • A user has speakers loud enough for the microphone to capture the sound.
    • Ask the user to turn their audio down or wear headphones.
  • A user's speakers are too close to the microphone.
    • Ask the user to move their speakers or turn the audio down. Swapping to headphones is also an option.
  • A user's echo cancellation is failing (can be device or computer performance issues).
    • Ask the user to confirm their settings are correct. This fix could require a device restart.
  • A user has a poor-quality microphone.
    • Ask the user to use a different audio source.
  • Someone has multiple instances of the event open.
    • Ask the user to close out one of the instances or lower the volume.
  • Someone is not using headphones in a small space.
    • Ask the user to use headphones or lower their volume.
  • Multiple people in the event and also in the same location. 
    • Ask the users to wear headphones, lower the volume, or share a device for the event.
  • Background noise from a speaker's environment.
    • Ask the user to mute when they are not speaking. 

To troubleshoot this, a producer can mute active mics to find the source of the echo. Once identified, you will need to connect with the user via chat (GR or DMs is your best bet) and ask them to resolve on their end. One of the quickest fixes (and easiest ways to avoid this issue in the first place) is to ask your speakers to use headphones for the event. Please note that if your speakers switch to headphones mid-event, they may need to adjust their A/V settings and/or refresh the page; it's best to troubleshoot these issues in the Green Room whenever possible.

Multi-Track Guide

A multi-track event will allow you to have multiple concurrent stages and areas under a single landing page. This guide is intended to show you how to create, manage, and navigate multi-track events. Before you start this guide, you'll want to be familiar with the Event Dashboard, please refer back to the feature guide here if needed.

The registration page will transform from a single-stage view to a multi-stage view. You'll be able to scroll down to see the different stages, along with their agendas. This allows your attendees to have a better view of the entire event from a glance.

How to Create a Multi-Track Event

  1. Create a new event in your organizational Dashboard.
  2. Once created, scroll to the bottom of your navigational panel on the left and click "Turn into multi-track event". Note that you can only do this if your event's start time is in the future.
  3. Fill out the fields for the name of the 2nd stage you want to create and click "Turn on Multi-Track". You will be able to add more stages to the navigation panel after it has been created.
  4. Congrats your event is now a multi-track event and now you will see new options in your navigational panel of the Event Dashboard.

Managing your Multi-Track Event

In the newly created multi-track event you will now see the two stages that have been created in the navigation panel on the left. One is the main stage which inherits details from your Event Details. Your new stage will not inherit these details and is a blank slate for you to customize. 

Each stage is self-contained and will have its own unique details, agenda, stage media, and streaming settings. Let's dive into each of the sections and explore what you can do.

Stage Details

You have three specific sections you can modify:

Details

  • Event Name - The name of the event is limited to 60 characters.
  • Event Description - This is the main body of text on the event registration page. Be aware of 300 character limit, if you go beyond the limit it will interfere with the scaling of your registration page image.
  • Start and End Time - Sets the time of the event. When setting the start time you are unable to set it to a time in the past or have the end time finish before the start time.
  • Time Zone - This will set the default time zone for your event.

Customization

Spaces

Allows editing the names of the "Spaces" that your users will be participating in.

  • Auditorium - Name of the main stage.
  • Breakouts - Name of the private/curated rooms that require invites by the producers. Please check out our Breakout Room Guide for further details.
  • Lounges - Name of the casual rooms attendees can jump in and out at their leisure. Please check out our Lounge Guide for further details.

Primary/Secondary Info Tabs

  • This creates additional tabs in the event that are linked to website URLs that are then embedded into the event.
  • The website you are linking to will need "iframing" for it to work. Most large public websites will not have this option on so you won't be able to link them. You can use https://iframetester.com/ to check if your website is compatible. 
  • When adding the URL please do not include HTTPS:// as it's already included. Adding this a second time will cause the page to not load.
  • Check out our Info Tab Guide for complete details.

Brand Details

Images

  • Logo - Appears at the top left corner of the registration page.
  • Icon - Appears at the top left corner of the event page. Please note that this is not visible in the producer's view.
  • Cover Image - Primary image that will appear next to your event/stage description for your event landing pages.
  • Please read our article regarding Graphics and Video Dimensions if you have questions on recommended sizing for these images.

Agenda

You will be able to build an agenda that can be accessed within the platform for your attendees. The agenda can be found on both the landing page and within the event. The agenda will showcase time ranges, session name, and a brief description of what the session will be. Please check out our Agenda Guide for specific details.

Stage Media

This is the area where you will upload your media assets, it is the ideal place to upload them in bulk. You are still be able to upload assets via the Control Room if needed.

Files are sorted alphabetically from top to bottom:

  • Background Images - These appear behind your speakers and slides, or as interstitials in between different sessions. 16:9 1920x1080; PNG, JPEG, GIF, SVG
  • Overlay Images - You can put overlays on stage to provide supplemental information and branding. 16:9 1920x1080; PNG, GIF, SVG
  • Logos - These appear unobtrusively in the bottom right corner of the stage. Keep these to 200x200, PNG
  • Videos - Videos can be interspersed with live feeds of your speakers. 1GB limit, 16:9 ratio, 720p, MP4
  • Music- Long audio files (greater than 8 seconds) to be played during your event. MP3 only
  • Sound Effects - Short audio files (less than 8 seconds) to be played during your event. MP3 only

Streaming

This section will allow you to setup up to 5 RTMP out streaming services. We have 4 built in options, Twitch, Youtube, Facebook, and Custom RTMP. You can follow our article Streaming Out - General Guide for further details.

Adding and Removing Stages

Adding a stage can be quickly done by clicking the + sign to the right of Stages in the navigational panel. This will generate a new stage below the others you have.

Removing a stage can be done within the "Stage Details" section of the stage at the very bottom. Simply click the red "Delete Stage" button and confirm the decision in the pop-up. Please note that you cannot delete the main stage. Also, any deleted stages are not retrievable.

How to Edit your Dynamic Link

Our quick registration link contains dynamic fields that you will need to make sure is compatible with the marketing platform you are using. Let's break down the dynamic link and give you some examples of the commonly used platforms.

https://app.experiencewelcome.com/events/EventID/quick_reg?email={email}&firstName={firstName}&lastName={lastName}

In this example of what the dynamic link would look like, we have highlighted the dynamic areas in red. These areas are the dynamic sections and will need to be made compatible with your marketing platform. What your marketing platform will do is auto-fill in these areas for you when you send out emails to your prospective users. 

Be sure to use your platforms hyperlink functions and not simply copy and paste. In some cases you may need to manually enter them by editing the link.

Let's go over some of the default fields of some of the more popular marketing platforms. This will not be an exhaustive list and you should always confirm with their respective resources. 

Marketo

Marketo calls them Tokens. Be sure to include them in the link settings.

{{lead.Email Address}}

{{lead.First Name}}

{{lead.Last Name}}

EXAMPLE: https://app.experiencewelcome.com/events/EventID/quick_reg?email={{lead.Email Address}}&firstName={{lead.First Name}}&lastName={{lead.Last Name}}

Splash

Splash calls them Personalization Tags. Be sure to include them in the link settings.

[email]

[first_name]

[last_name}

EXAMPLE: https://app.experiencewelcome.com/events/EventID/quick_reg?email=[email]&firstName=[first_name]&lastName=[last_name]

Hubspot

Hubspot uses Personalization Tokens and these need to be inserted through the Text Editor UI.

Salesforce/Pardot

Pardot calls them Variable Tags and you may need to create these fields yourself depending on how your environment is set up. Please refer to Pardot support articles for specifics.

How to Enable this Feature

In the event dashboard, using the left panel, navigate to Event Details, scroll down to the Registration Flow section, click on the box next to Enable Quick Registration, then click Save.

Things to be Aware of

You should not add attendees whom you want to use quick registration links into the Welcome Attendees Tab, manually, via an integration, or CSV upload. This feature will NOT work for Speakers, please pre-register your speakers and send them One-Click Links.

Rather, you should send out quick-registration links in emails that come from your marketing automation tool and let the attendee's action of clicking the quick registration link load them into the Welcome attendee tab automatically.

Quick registration links will not take over regular Welcome registrant accounts that get loaded into the Attendees Tab or those that have an existing Welcome profile. So, if a person attempts to use a quick reg link for an existing event registrant, they will be redirected to the event landing page to log in as that registrant.

Lastly, "Email Allow List" should not be used in conjunction with Quick Registration, as the two functionalities will conflict with each other.

How to Screen Share

To start screen sharing you will need to either be inside the Green Room or in one of our Lounge/Breakout rooms. When in the those rooms you should see a button with this logo and may say "Present".

You should receive a pop up with the different screen share options for Chrome

Screen Share Options

You will have 3 options to choose from

  • Entire Screen - This will allow you to select entire desktops. If you have multiple monitors you will be given the option to select either or. You are also given the option at the bottom left to toggle on "share system audio"
  • Window - Allows you to select an open application window
  • Chrome Tab - Allows you to select an open tab in Chrome. You are also given the option at the bottom left to toggle on "share audio" for that tab.

Depending on what you are trying to share the behaviors of each option may vary. 

Screen Sharing Slide Presentations

Let's go over two of the most used slide presentation software for Windows PC. Ideally, it is best to have access to two monitors when screen sharing a presentation. One monitor would be used for the presentation and the other monitor to view the green room and chat interaction panel. If you do not have access to a secondary monitor that is ok too we will guide you with some best practices for that.

Microsoft PowerPoint and Google Slides happen to be the most popular. They both are the same but how you share them can be quite different. You will have further differences if you use only one monitor versus two.

Screen Sharing Microsoft PowerPoint

This is the most popular slide presentation software used for presentations. Based on if you have one or more monitors will vary the way you setup the presentation. A two monitor setup is the most ideal since it will allow you to see both your slides and the Welcome event in full.

1 Monitor

In this method we will be sharing the application window within Chrome. Sadly, due to how Chrome and PowerPoint interact you may have black bar at the right and left of you screen share on the stage. Google Slide behaves much better than PowerPoint for 1 monitor screen sharing. The reason for this method is so the speaker can both see the presentation they are giving and the Welcome stage at the same time. It is especially good if the speaker is interested in seeing what sort of engagement is happening in the chat. 

Video walkthrough here:

First thing you want to do is resize you windows so that you can see both the presentation software and Welcome Event

In PowerPoint, click "Slide Show", then click "Set Up Slide Show"

Select "Browsed by an individual (window)" and click OK

While still in "Slide Show" tab, click "Play from Start" on the left

You may need to resize the PowerPoint window to be about 2/3rds of your screen (might take some trial and error here depending on your screen size)

Click "Present" within Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner)

Choose "Application Window" tab > select the PowerPoint window > click "Share"

From here you may need to resize your two windows (PowerPoint and Welcome) into whatever is comfortable for you. Note that you'll still need to keep the PowerPoint window fairly big as if you try to make it smaller it will resize your presentation within the Welcome stage.

2 Monitors

This is the easiest method to manage two different windows. On monitor will contain the presentation at full screen and the 2nd monitor will contain the Welcome event window. This will also result in a cleaner presentation for PowerPoint due to using "Your Entire Screen" rather than "Application Window".

Video walkthrough here:

First thing you will need to do is move your presentation to one monitor and your Welcome Window to the second monitor.

By default your presentation should go straight to full screen but if it doesn't. Within PowerPoint Click on the "Slide Show" tab, then click on "Set Up Slide Show"

Choose "Presented by a speaker (full screen)"

While still in "Slide Show" tab, click "Play from Start" on the left. This should make the presentation cover the whole screen.

In the other monitor, Click "Present" on Welcome (it will be towards the bottom left-hand corner)

Choose the appropriate screen under "Your Entire Screen" tab. 

The presentation needs to be the active window for you to change slides. Be sure the presentation is the last thing you clicked on.

Screen Sharing Google Slides

Screen sharing with Google Slides within Chrome is the easiest way and less problematic way of screen sharing a presentation. Unlike PowerPoint which requires either sharing from desktop or application window you will be sharing from a Chrome tab. This results in a cleaner and easier presentation due to Chrome and Slides being in the same Google ecosystem.

1 Monitor

In this method we will be sharing from a Chrome tab. For 1 monitor presentations, this behaves better than if you were to use PowerPoint. You may want to consider uploading your PowerPoint presentation to Google slides and run it this way. The reason for this method is so the speaker can both see the presentation they are giving and the Welcome stage at the same time. It is especially good if the speaker is interested in seeing what sort of engagement is happening in the chat.

Video walkthrough here:

Have your Google Slides presentation loaded up in a separate window from Welcome. Once you have been invited and entered the Green Room, click "Present" button on the lower left-hand corner.

Choose "Chrome Tab" at the top, then your Google Slides presentation. Click "Share" to complete the process. Please note that you can check the "Share audio" check box in the bottom left if you intend to include audio in your presentation.

Now go to your Google Slides presentation Window. You can either go to "View" tab, then click "Slideshow". Or you can simply press "Slideshow" on the upper right-hand corner. You can then resize/move the application window to your liking while also being able to see the Welcome platform. You can use arrow keys to change slides.

2 Monitor

This is the easiest method to manage two different windows. One monitor will contain the presentation  and the 2nd monitor will contain the Welcome event window. This way you don't have to worry about managing the different windows covering each other up. The biggest difference between 1 monitor and 2 monitor sharing for Google Slides is simply moving the application windows to the different monitors. The rest of the steps is exactly the same.

First thing you will need to do is move your presentation to one monitor and your Welcome Window to the second monitor.

Once you have been invited and entered the Green Room, click "Present" button on the lower left-hand corner.

Choose "Chrome Tab" at the top, then your Google Slides presentation. Click "Share" to complete the process. Please note that you can check the "Share audio" check box in the bottom left if you intend to include audio in your presentation.

Now go to your Google Slides presentation Window. You can either go to "View" tab, then click "Slideshow". Or you can simply press "Slideshow" on the upper right-hand corner. You can then resize/move the application window to your liking and you can use arrow keys to change slides.

Window Sizing

For the best experience on Welcome, we strongly recommend that your window size is fairly large. This is important because Welcome resizes itself according to the size of your window and depending on the size, you may experience some loss of functionality in this process. 

This information is most important for producers as you will need an adequately sized window to control all of the functions in the control room. Attendees and Speakers should also strive to have well-sized windows as there is some resizing on Welcome's end that can impact their experience as well.

There are a few methods for resizing your window:

  • Going full screen.
  • Use your browser's zoom in/out feature.
  • Resize your window by dragging the edge of the window.

With that said, it's also important to find a setup that works well for you and you are comfortable with. Below is a breakdown of what we'd recommend by resizing options.

  • If you have multiple monitors or an easy method to change applications (such as the three-finger drag on Macs or the Taskbar on PCs), full screen is a great option to ensure your window is appropriately sized. If you've got screen real estate, this is the way to go as you're much less likely to run into accidental resizing issues that can impact an event.
  • If you are limited to a smaller screened device or have no second monitor, you will probably want to not go full screen and just have a larger window. With this setup, you are able to have other apps you need handy on the desktop for quick switching. Be mindful of accidentally resizing your window if you go this route though.
  • Zooming should primarily be used for more granular changes to your setup. You will still want a large window or a full-screened window to ensure that Welcome hasn't resized and functionality is being blocked.

Going fullscreen

If you have multiple monitors, we recommend using fullscreen for the best experience whenever possible. This will allow you to have a large Welcome window and any other applications you need open (such as an internal communication tool like Slack). This will allow you to maximize your screen space and have less of a chance of resizing the window and leading to a poor experience. While in full screen, you can also utilize your browser's zoom feature for more granular resizing of the event. We recommend you stick close to 100% zoom for the best experience.

Zooming your browser in and out can also resize Welcome. Using this you can achieve a well-sized window or make granular changes to your setup by combining this with full screen or window resizing.

Agenda Builder Guide

Events have a lot of moving parts to sort and organize. We want to make it simple process and have all the important details in one location for you. We created the Agenda Builder in the Control Panel to do just that. This guide is going to take you through how the Agenda Builder functions.

  • What is Agenda Builder
  • How to Use Agenda Builder
  • Adding Sessions
  • Adding Assets
  • Managing your Sessions and Assets

What is Agenda Builder

Agenda Builder is where you will spend most of your time getting your event organized before the event and during the event. This allows you to generate a central source of truth for your event as a producer.

Agenda Builder serves multiple functions:

  1. Create and manage your event agenda
  2. Upload and organize your assets
  3. Access to Stagekit, allows you to create assets on the platform. Such as editable overlays, backgrounds, polls, and videos.
  4. Create cue notes
  5. Pushing assets on and off the stage

Now that you have a basic understanding of what the Agenda Builder is, let’s dive into how to use it.

How to use Agenda Builder

Agenda Builder can be as simple and as complex as you would like it to be. This guide is going to show you how to use the basic functions. You can then use your creativity and event use case to cater towards your needs.

Agenda Builder is located on the left panel of the Control Room. At first glance it will be pretty bare, let’s dive in and show you how to fill it up with goodies. You will only see a session named “Unassigned Media”. This session tab will be where you can store assets that may not need to be put into a specific session such as a “Technical difficulties” background/overlay.

Adding Sessions

Sessions make up the macro view of the Agenda Builder and represents what the agenda attendees see. Start by organizing your event by sessions then go back through and fill those sessions with the micro details.

You can start by adding a session by clicking on the “Add session” button at the top right of the Agenda Builder. You will be asked to fill out some fields:

  • Title
  • Description
  • Session durations

Once the session has been created you can edit it by clicking on the 3 stacked dots on the right side of the session name.

Adding Assets

Clicking “Add” in your session will give you a pop up to add a variety of assets.

  • Stage Kit - allows you to use our premade assets within Welcome. Please check out our Stage Kit Guide for specific details.
    • Video - stock videos 
    • Audio - stock music
    • Overlays - create editable transparent and non transparent overlays
      • The Agenda overlay will automatically update based on your sessions you have in your Agenda Builder
    • Backgrounds - collection of static and animated backgrounds based on your brand detail colors. 
    • Polls - pre-made polls
  • Upload - Use your own custom assets
  • Add Poll - Add either your own custom Poll or use one of our pre-made polls
  • Add Cue Note - Flexible tool that you can use to write notes to hand off to others, remind yourself of important things. When to perform tasks, invites, links, open lounge, etc. You are also able to assign colors to cue notes for better organization.

Managing your Sessions and Assets

Rarely will you ever have everything set correctly the first time. We made it easy to manage the Agenda Builder. You can simply drag and drop your assets and sessions around. You are also able to collapse/expand sessions by clicking on the arrow to the left of the session name.

Editing your assets can be simply done by hovering over it and a popup will appear in the top left corner with these options:

  • Rename
  • Duplicate
  • Delete
  • Edit
  • Cue Card Color

Event Agenda

As you build your event the agenda will automatically update on its own. You can see the agenda by clicking the Agenda Icon at the top right corner of the event next to your profile icon. Clicking it will expand the agenda at the right of the event.
